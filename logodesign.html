Logo Design & Branding

Where Vision Meets Visual Identity

About Us

At TwicTech, we design logos that are more than just symbols — they’re strategic brand assets that communicate your identity, build trust, and leave a lasting impression. Whether you're launching a new venture or rebranding an established business, our logo design process is built to bring your story to life — creatively and cohesively.

Why a Great Logo Matters

Your logo is often the first thing people notice — and the last thing they remember. It should reflect your values, speak to your audience, and align with your brand’s personality. A great logo isn't just beautiful — it's purposeful, scalable, and memorable.

What Makes Our Logo Design Unique?.

At TwicTech, we combine branding intelligence with design thinking and digital compatibility. We understand that logos must work online and offline, across mobile apps websites, print, and social media — making our approach future-ready and technology-aware.

Logo Design Process

We follow a proven, collaborative, and insight-driven process:

•

Brand Discovery

We begin with a deep dive into your business — values, audience, competition, and vision. This helps us define the right tone, shape, and visual language.

•

Creative Strategy

Based on insights, we create a strategic foundation: mood boards, color psychology, font style, icon ideas, and visual direction.

•

Concept Development

Our designers present multiple logo concepts (wordmarks, icons, emblems, etc.), each crafted to convey your brand’s essence.

•

Feedback & Revisions

We refine the selected concept based on your input — adjusting typography, colors, spacing, and scalability.

•

Final Delivery

You receive a complete logo package, including:

  • • High-resolution files (PNG, SVG, PDF, JPEG)
  • • Black & white and color variations
  • • Vertical and horizontal lockups
  • • Brand usage guidelines (optional)

Types of Logos We Create

We customize logo design based on your brand goals:

Wordmark Logos

Clean, text-based logos using stylized fonts (e.g., Google, TwicTech)

Lettermark Logos

Initials or monograms (e.g., IBM, HP)

Pictorial Marks

Icon or graphic-based symbols (e.g., Apple, Twitter)

Abstract Logos

Geometric shapes and visuals (e.g., Pepsi, Adidas)

Combination Marks

Text + symbol for maximum flexibility

Emblem Logos

Badge-style logos (great for educational, government, or classic brands)
